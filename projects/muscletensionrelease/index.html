Exercise Variations
Paired breathing

Combine deep breathing techniques with your tense and release. Deep breathing can help you practice the Box Breathing Method.

Paired wording

It can be helpful to say a certain word or phrase to yourself as you slowly exhale (such as “relax”, “let go”, “stay calm”, “peace”, “it will pass” etc.). This word or phrase will become associated with a relaxed state; eventually, saying this word alone can bring on a calm feeling.

Release Only

Instead of tensing your muscles before relaxing them, try just the relaxation part. This can also increase your general awareness of any tension, sensation, or emotion.

Laying down

Lay on your back on a comfortable surface. A yoga mat on a hard surface is perfect. You be the judge. Complete the same exercises, but substitute as appropiate (e.g., pointing toes and stretching calves instead of pressing toes into the mud).

Exercise Instructions

Each muscle group counts as one "set", which has two parts:

1. Tense (10 seconds): Tense/Flex the glowing muscle groups as hard as you can for at least 4 of the 10 seconds.
2. Release (20 seconds): Let go and breathe. Relax.

At the end of the exercise, you have the option to repeat your entire personalized exercise.

Developed to be completed standing or sitting. Adjust exercises accordingly if you prefer laying down. Practice for 10-20 minutes every other day.

About this web application

This muscle tension release project is a basic visual guide for an adapted Progressive Muscle Relaxation procedure. Visitors may select a realistic body image model, colours of the body and glow, and order of relaxation.

This exercise will take 1 to 3 minutes depending on your customization. A skip is provided during the actual exercise for your convenience.
